About Me

2023-01-02

Who am I?

My name is Bruce A. Lee, I’m a Data Analyst from Florida, and also an alumni of the University of Central Florida. My analytical toolset comprises of SQL (postgreSQL), R Programming (for data cleaning, visualizations, and statistical output), Tableau and Excel; with working adoption of Python.

My career has been an interesting one. I’ve been fortunate enough to have been able to work in several roles across many different industries—while finding myself in situations that have molded me into the professional I am today. I’ve come a very far away from the 17 year old kid from Ocala, FL, whose first ever job was working at McDonalds out of high school.

Most of my experience lives within the Education space, over the last 8 years, I’ve taken up various data-centric roles. I’ve worked in two prominent and nationally scaled Education start-ups; as well as one of the top 5 largest Education SaaS companies in the US; i’ve also worked in non-profits for City Year AmeriCorps, and a DOE/DJJ/DOL funded juvenile justice rehabilitation program.

My desire to work in data came out of a realization that many of the statistical methods that I learned from my undergrad, formed most of the skills that data analysts use day to day. I knew that I could understand data, and that perhaps maybe I was selling myself short. So I put myself to work in learning R with a friend’s help, while studying the base concepts of what it means to work with tidy and messy data after my day job. From there, I started to learn SQL and things started to come together, as I’d been using Microsoft Excel in my work for a few years up until then.

Why hire me?

I’m extremely persistent for starters. Once I set my sights on something, I do not let up and until I can feel satisfied that my job was completed and that my stakeholders are happy with the outcome. I also find that my communication style engages people into action, I like asking intentional questions to uncover the intentions behind why a decision was made, so as to fine-tune an approach or pivot to a different one.

I love to unravel how an organization works, so that I can evaluate it and improve it in my own way. When it comes to understanding a business, I try to see why the need for certain outcomes are important to growing an org, through looking at its data. I find that doing so can uncover troubling patterns that I can then route out and provide recommendations for solving in terms of problems.

Lastly, I find that the most successful analysts understands their audience, because everyone is different and you have to be adaptable enough to know what storytelling style suits the situation best. I enjoy getting to know the people I’m working with and under, understanding their communication style and how to work with them.
