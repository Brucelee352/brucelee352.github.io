About Me

2023-01-02

Who am I?

My name is Bruce Anthony Lee, I’m a Data Analyst from Florida, and also an alumni of the University of Central Florida. My analytical toolset comprises of SQL (postgreSQL), R Programming (for data cleaning, visualizations, and statistical output), Tableau and Excel; with working adopting of Python.

My career I must say has been a fruitful one, with my victories, failures — ups and down. I’ve been fortunate enough to have been able to experience many varied situations between several different industries, situations that have molded me into the professional I am today. I’ve come a very far away since the 17 year old kid from Ocala, FL; whose first ever job was working at McDonalds.

The breadth of my experience lives within the Education space, in various data-centric roles for the past few years since then; after taking on several odd jobs during my college years.

I’ve worked in two prominent and nationally scaled Education start-ups; as well as one of the top 5 largest Education SaaS companies in the US; i’ve also worked in non-profits for City Year AmeriCorps and DOE/DJJ/DOL funded companies.

My desire to work in data came out of a realization that during my time in my Sociology program in undergrad, many of the statistical methods that I learned formed a good plethora of the skills that data analysts use day to day. I figured that there was no reason why I couldn’t leverage what I knew to work on projects that I was interested in. I wanted to work towards bettering outcomes in Education, so the work I ended up taking on projects centered around gleaning insights from education data.

So why hire me? Well, I’m extremely persistent for starters. Once I set my sights on something, I do not let up and until I can feel satisfied that my job was completed and that my stakeholders are happy with the outcomes. I also find that my communication style engages people into action, I like asking intentional questions to uncover the intentions behind why a decision was made, so as to fine-tune an approach or pivot to a different one.

I find that the most successful analysts understands their audience, because everyone is different and you have to be adaptable enough to know what storytelling style suits the situation best.
